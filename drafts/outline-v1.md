1. Statement of Question and Hypotheses

The cultural and political context facing mainline Protestant congregations has shifted rapidly and unexpectedly in recent decades
Traditional institutional responses — sermons, liturgy, corporate worship — may be insufficient for helping congregations navigate this shift.

Primary hypothesis: Because reality is constructed through a social process, sermons and rituals are effective in supporting an established understanding of reality, but book and discussion groups are more effective when a faith community is experiencing rapid and unanticipated changes in its cultural setting.

Secondary hypothesis: The rise of White Christian Nationalism illustrates what happens when church leaders follow the social construction of reality that members and potential members from an emerging cultural transformation. 
thus the plausibility structures are resourced by the culture without reference to recent conservative and progressive theological paradigm.

Research question: How does Arvada UMC use different communication forms to construct and maintain shared religious meaning during a period of rapid cultural change?
